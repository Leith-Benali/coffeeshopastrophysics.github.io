Upcoming Events


We will be holding lectures at Anodyne Coffee on the following dates starting at 1pm. Each event typically lasts 45-60 minutes, and questions are encouraged! Please see a list of the planned and past events below. We will also be hosting livestreamed lectures on our YouTube Channel (link below).

CoffeeShop Astrophysics YouTube Channel


2/25/23: The Universe is Not Real – Pratyusava Baral and Gabe Freedman


    Perhaps you’ve heard the news. The 2022 Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded for experiments proving that the universe is not ‘locally real’. That might seem… unsettling. What does it mean for something to be real anyways? Come join us at Anodyne, we’ll all get through this together.


3/11/23: How Do We “See” a Black Hole? – Ronan Humphrey, Allie Spaulding, and Lulu Agazie


    If black holes don’t emit light, how do we see them and know that they are there? We can do this by studying how they affect matter nearby! Come learn about multiple techniques used to detect these “dark” objects!


4/22/23: The Lifespan of Stars – Shashwat Sardesai and Tamal RoyChowdhury


From tiny stars that will last to the end of time, to massive stars that burn bright and brief, we explore the life and death of stars. We discuss the chaos brewing within the fiery furnace of a star and what fates lie in wait for it.


5/6/23: The Science of Science Fiction – Sarah Borges and Simon Yeung

    Join us for an epic journey into the science behind your favorite sci-fi movies! Discover the secrets of time with the blockbusters Back to the Future, Interstellar, Arrival, and Tenet. From the weirdness of relativity and time dilation to entropy, you'll explore the fascinating nature of time in this CoffeeShop Astrophysics event. Don't miss your chance to join us in this expedition behind the fiction!
